Canada Pension Plan enhancement

The Canada Pension Plan (CPP) is a retirement plan you contribute to during your earning years. You contribute to the CPP from your annual income between a minimum and maximum amount.

On January 1, 2019, individuals started contributing more to the Canada Pension Plan (CPP). We introduced this change, known as the CPP enhancement, to help workers increase their retirement income.

Contributions will increase for most individuals in 2024.

What the CPP enhancement is

In 2019, we introduced the CPP enhancement to increase retirement benefits significantly. This was to help individuals save enough for retirement. We rolled out the enhancement in two phases.

The first phase (2019-2023) saw incremental increases in contribution rate:

  • employees and employers went from 4.95% to 5.95%
  • self-employed individuals went from 9.9% to 11.9%

Phase 2: 2024-2025

In 2024, the CPP contribution rate will still be:

  • employees and employers went from 4.95% to 5.95%
  • 5.95% for employers and employees each
  • 11.9% for self-employed individuals (the sum of what an employee and an employer would contribute)

The amount you pay is based on your income. Income over a certain amount (a “ceiling”) is subject to a higher contribution rate. The first earning ceiling for 2022 is $64,900. Starting on January 1, 2024, we'll introduce a second higher ceiling called the year's additional maximum pensionable earnings. Phase 2 of the CPP enhancement will mainly affect individuals with a higher annual income.

The amount for the second ceiling is:

  • based on the first ceiling
  • set at 7% higher than the first ceiling for the first year
  • due to increase to 14% in 2025 and the following years

If you earn above both ceilings, you'll contribute on the difference between the two ceilings:

  • the maximum pensionable earnings (first ceiling) and
  • the additional maximum pensionable Earning (second ceiling)

If you earn more than the first ceiling but less than the second ceiling, you'll contribute on the difference between your earnings and maximum pensionable earnings.

If you earn less than the first ceiling, you'll contribute based on your earnings at that year's rate.

In 2024, the second earnings ceiling CPP contribution rates will be:

  • 4% for employers and employees each
  • 8% for self-employed individuals (the sum of what an employee and an employer would contribute)
Example: Employee (high-income earner)

Maya is a high-income earner. She earns $150,000, more than the first ceiling ($67,700 in 2024) and the second ceiling ($72,439). In addition to her first contribution, her enhanced contribution would be ($72,439 - $67,700) x 4% = $189.56:

  • $72,439 (second ceiling)
  • minus $67,700 (first ceiling)
  • multiplied by 2024's enhanced contribution rate of 4%
  • total enhanced annual contribution of $189.56
Example: Employee

Diego’s annual income is below the first earnings ceiling ($67,700), he will only make contributions on the difference between the minimum earnings of $3,500 and his annual earnings of $45,000. He would only contribute as per 2024's first ceiling rate. No additional contribution would be necessary. The calculation would be ($45,000 - $3,500) × 5.95% = $2,469:

  • $45,000 in annual earnings
  • minus $3,500 (ineligible deduction amount)
  • multiplied by 2024's first contribution rate of 5.95%
  • total annual contribution of $2,469
Example: Self-employed

Ryan is self-employed and earns $70,000, more than the first ceiling ($67,700) but less than the second ceiling ($72,439). In addition to his first contribution, his enhanced contribution would be ($70,000 - $67,700) x 8% = $184:

  • $70,000 in annual earnings
  • minus $67,700 (first ceiling)
  • multiplied by 2024's second contribution rate of 8%
  • total second annual contribution of $184
Employer

Employers match their employees’ enhanced (2024) contribution rate of 4%.
