Basic steps to claiming

To claim SR&ED tax incentives, you will need to identify your eligible SR&ED work and group it into a project, calculate your SR&ED expenditures, and then claim your investment tax credit (ITC).

You may submit your required SR&ED forms with your income tax return for the year.

Be sure to keep all your technical and financial information to support your claim.

Resources you’ll need

We recommend that businesses allow for the involvement of senior technical and financial personnel in the claiming process to help ensure the accuracy of the claim.

Why involve senior staff

Claiming SR&ED tax incentives requires detailed information about the type of work carried out, the reasons for doing it, and the methods used.

It also  involves determining  the allowable, deductible, and qualified expenditures incurred while doing the work and calculating the investment tax credit for the year.

Additionally, if a claim is selected for a detailed review, having the involvement of those with in depth knowledge of the work, as well as the company’s finances, may also help expediate the process.

To properly budget the time and resources needed to complete your claim, we encourage you to review the eligibility requirements before you begin.

Filing due dates

Submit your claim with your tax return

Your SR&ED claim can only be processed once the CRA has received your income tax return for the year.

To avoid delays, submit your SR&ED claim at the same time as your income tax return.

To process your claim, the CRA requires:

  • Income tax return

  • T661 SR&ED Expenditures Claim

  • T2SCH31 Investment tax credit
    or
    T2038(IND) Investment tax credit

  • Other forms
    (as needed)

Submit your claim before the deadline

The CRA does not accept any changes to a claim after the SR&ED reporting deadline has passed.

We recommend you submit your SR&ED claim earlier than the reporting deadline and together with your income tax return.

Why send it early

If you submit your claim at least 90 days before the SR&ED reporting deadline, it will give the CRA enough time to do an initial review. If any information is  missing, the CRA will have time to advise you, and you will have time to amend your claim before the deadline.

Due dates

The SR&ED reporting deadline depends on your income tax filing due date.

SR&ED reporting deadline:

12 months after your income tax filing due date for the year

Income tax filing due dates:

Self-employed Individuals

T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return is due June 15 of the following year

Keep your supporting records

Keeping proper documentation will make it easier to prepare and support your claim.

You must be able to support:

  • What work was done
  • Who was involved with the work
  • When they did the work
  • How you calculated the associated expenditures

If your claim is selected for a detailed review, this will help reviewers understand your request, the work done, and how expenses associated with the work were determined and calculated.

What records to keep

Keep any information that you feel will support your SR&ED claim.

Documentation that is dated, signed, and specific to the work performed is optimal, however we will accept various types of supporting records.

Examples of supporting records
  • Project planning documents
  • Documents on design of experiments
  • Experimentation plan
  • Design documents and technical drawings
  • Project records and laboratory notebooks
  • Design, system architecture, and source code (software development)
  • Records of trial runs
  • Project progress reports
  • Minutes of project meetings, virtual meeting chats
  • Whiteboard drawings
  • Test protocols, data, results, analysis, and conclusions
  • Final project report or professional publication
  • Photographs and videos
  • Prototypes and samples
  • Scrap and scrap records
  • Contracts and lease agreements
  • Records of resources allocated to the project, time sheets, activity records, and payroll records
  • Purchase invoices and proof of payment
  • Accounting records
  • Other artefacts that relate to the work

Review Documentation and other evidence to support your SR&ED claim: T4088 Appendix 2

Make sure your claim is accurate

Claimants are responsible for the accuracy of the information in their SR&ED claims, whether they complete the claim themselves or have it done by someone else.

We recommend being proactive with recordkeeping. Maintaining records after the fact is much harder and often leads to more mistakes.
