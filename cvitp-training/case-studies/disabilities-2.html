Background information

Situation

Alexei received CPP and OAS benefits while living in a nursing home for all of 2022. He pays $16,200 per year to the nursing home. The receipt provided by the nursing home is not broken down for meals, accommodation, or attendant care services. Alexei has been previously approved for the disability tax credit (DTC).

Takeaway points

Steps to follow

  1. Review their background information and required slips
  2. In Interview setup, tick the boxes next to Pension income, other income and split pension income, COVID-19 benefits (T4A, T4A(OAS), T4A(P), T4-RCA, T4RSP, T4RIF,T1032) in the Pension section and Medical expenses, disability, caregiver in the Common tax deductions section
  3. Click T4A and pension income in the left-side menu and click the + signs next to T4A(OAS), T4A(P) and Split pension income with spouse to enter the amounts for each tax slip
  4. Alexei’s T4A(P) indicates he received both CPP retirement and disability benefits throughout the year; the sum of box 23 and box 21 (number of months) cannot exceed 12, so enter a 6 into both the box 23 and box 21 fields
  5. Click Medical, disability, caregiver in the left-side menu and click the + sign next to Medical expenses in the Medical expenses section
  6. Click Medical expenses in the left-side menu to enter the nursing home fees next to the Fees for a residential and long-term care centre field
Since Alexei qualifies for the disability amount, he has the option to claim the disability amount or the nursing home fees, but not both. In this situation, it is more beneficial to claim the nursing home fees as a medical expense instead of the disability tax credit. If you enter both the disability tax credit and medical expenses for a residential and long-term care centre into the software for the same tax return, the software does not claim the DTC.
