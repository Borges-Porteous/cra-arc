Determine if a benefit is taxable

Generally, fees for counselling services or income tax preparation you provide or pay on behalf of your employee are taxable. Depending on your situation, the benefit from counselling services may not be taxable under the Income Tax Act (ITA) exemption.

If the benefit is related to amounts paid for using recreational or sporting facilities and club dues, refer to: Recreational facilities and club dues

Situations

Situation: You pay for counselling services related to the mental or physical health of the taxpayer or related individual

Non-taxable situation

Under the Income Tax Act (ITA) exemption, the benefit is not taxable if it is for counselling services in respect of the physical or mental health of your employee or a person related to the employee.

Counselling services include:

  • Dealing with addictions, such as tobacco, drug and alcohol abuse
  • Stress management
  • Employee assistance programs
Examples

  • Not taxable: Option 1

    • You decide to pay for counselling on behalf of any staff member who would like to stop smoking. Your employee decides to take the employer-paid counselling. The benefit is not taxable in this situation.
    • You offer a range of counselling services to your employees. This includes workshops related to nutrition, exercise, wellness and mental health that are open to all employees. Your employees may also book a one-on-one session with a health-care professional, who bills you for their services. An employee attends four workshops and the employee also booked a session with a dietician that you paid for. The benefit is not taxable in this situation.

  • Taxable: Option 2

    • You offer a range of counselling services to your employees. Your employees may book sessions with a range of service providers. An employee hires a personal trainer for ten sessions. You reimburse the employee for half the cost. The benefit is taxable in this situation.

Taxable situation

If the fees paid for counselling services are not for the physical or mental health of your employee or a related person, the benefit is taxable.

To calculate the benefit, see: Calculate the value of the benefit

Situation: You pay for the cost of counselling services related to the re-employment of your employee

Non-taxable situation

Under the Income Tax Act (ITA) exemption, the benefit is not taxable if it for counselling services in respect of the employee's re-employment with you.

Taxable situation

If the fees paid for counselling services are for employment with another employer or include a financial counselling component, the benefit is taxable.

For example, retraining of an employee in the event of termination where it is not primarely for the benefit of the employer is a taxable benefit.

To calculate the benefit, see: Calculate the value of the benefit

Examples

  • Not taxable: Option 1

    • As part of a termination package for a long time employee, the employer arranges for them to attend another two-day counselling seminar on job search techniques and resume writing skills. The benefit is not taxable in this situation.
    • A downsizing company is removing several of its order fulfillment staff. Employees are being offered other positions with the company, but must first complete appropriate training programs. An employee is offered a sales position, but first must complete a 3-week program. The full cost is paid by the employer. The benefit is not taxable in this situation.

  • Taxable: Option 2

    • Included in a severance package offered to a long term employee is an offer to cover costs of retraining for prospective employment given that the employee is no longer employed due to downsizing. The re-training is not for the employer's benefit. The benefit is taxable in this situation.
Situation: You pay for the cost of counselling services related to the retirement of your employee

Non-taxable situation

Under the Income Tax Act (ITA) exemption, the benefit is not taxable if it for counselling services in respect of the employee's retirement.

Taxable situation

If the fees paid for retirement counselling services include financial counselling or tax preparation, the benefit is taxable.

To calculate the benefit, see: Calculate the value of the benefit

Examples

  • Not taxable: Option 1

    • A company is downsizing due to declining sales. It is offering a retirement package to an employee who has been employed with the company as a salesperson for 25 years. The retirement package includes a one-day counselling seminar to prepare for retirement. The benefit is not taxable in this situation.

  • Taxable: Option 2

    • A company offers all employees with at least 35 years of service discounted counselling upon retirement. The counselling is mainly related to investment and financial planning. A retired employee receives an amount for counselling in the year, but is required to pay a portion for the service. The benefit is taxable in this situation.
Situation: You pay for services related to financial counselling or income tax preparation

The benefit does not qualify for the Income Tax Act (ITA) exemption. The benefit is taxable.

Examples

Example of services that do not qualify for the exemption

  • You provide income tax preparation for personal tax returns or personal tax planning
  • You provide services through your accounting firm, or your employee or shareholder is reimbursed for the expense
  • You provide services for the preparation of out-of-country tax returns for an employee or shareholder

To calculate the benefit, see: Calculate the value of the benefit

Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

If the benefit is taxable, you must withhold the following deductions. The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

The withholding and remitting requirement depends on the type of remuneration: cash , non-cash , or near-cash .

  • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

    Withhold:

    • Income tax
    • CPP
    • EI (do not withhold)

    Remit:

    • GST/HST

  • Cash: Option 2

    Withhold:

    • Income tax
    • CPP
    • EI

    Remit:

    • GST/HST - Reimbursements
    • GST/HST - Allowances (do not remit)

Report the benefit on a slip

If the benefit is taxable, you must report the following on the T4 slip.

  • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

    Report on:

    • Box 14 - Employment Income
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
    • Code 40 - Other Information

  • Cash: Option 2

    Report on:

    • Box 14 - Employment Income
    • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
    • Code 40 - Other Information
