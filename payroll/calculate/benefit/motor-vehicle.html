Determine if a benefit is taxable

Generally, if your employee uses a motor vehicle to perform their duties of office or employment it is a taxable benefit. Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the CRA's administrative policy .

Situation: Motor vehicle you provide to your employee

Non-taxable situation

Under the CRA's administrative policy, if you provide your employee with a motor vehicle that you own or lease and your employee uses the motor vehicle for business use only, the benefit is not taxable.

Examples of business use and personal use

The personal use is when the employee's use of a motor vehicle provided by the employer or a person related to the employer, for purposes other than the employer's business.

  • Business use

    • Travel from office workplace to client workplace (and vice versa)
    • Travel from office workplace for errands for business purposes (and vice versa)
    • Travel from home to client workplace (and vice versa)

  • Personal use

    • Travel from office workplace to home (and vice versa), even if the employer insist the employee to drive the vehicle home, for example:
      • the employee is on 24 hour call
      • the employer feels the security of the vehicle and its contents is better served at the employee's residence
      • it is more convenient or economical to have the vehicle driven to the employee's residence
    • Vacation trips

Taxable situation

If you provide your employee with a motor vehicle that you own or lease and your employee uses the motor vehicle for both business and personal purposes, the personal use is a taxable benefit. Generally, the benefits are for leasing expenses and operating costs.

Situation: Allowances you provide to your employee for their motor vehicle expenses

Non-taxable situation

Under the Income Tax Act (ITA) exemption, if you paid an allowance for motor vehicle expenses to your employee, the benefit is not taxable if all of the following apply:

  • The allowance is based only on the number of business kilometres (in connection with or during the course of the office or employment) driven in a year
  • The rate per-kilometre is reasonable, meaning:
    • Rate matches the prescribed rate
    • If rate does not match the prescribed rate, you can justify your position
  • The total allowance was paid in a per-kilometre rate
  • You did not reimburse the employee for expenses related to the same use of the vehicle, except where the reimbursement is in respect of supplementary business insurance or toll or ferry charges and the amount of the allowance was determined without reference to those reimbursed expenses

Taxable situation

If the allowance provided to your employee for motor vehicle expenses do not meet all of the conditions above, the benefit is taxable.

For example, if the employee both received an allowance in respect of their use of the motor vehicle and is reimbursed in whole or in part for expenses in respect of that use (except where the reimbursement is in respect of supplementary business insurance or toll or ferry charges and the amount of the allowance was determined without reference to those reimbursed expenses).

What are the reasonable motor vehicle allowance rates

The motor vehicle allowance rates for 2022 are:

  • 61¢ per kilometre for the first 5,000 kilometres driven
  • 55¢ per kilometre driven after that
    In the Northwest Territories, Yukon, and Nunavut, there is an additional 4¢ per kilometre allowed for travel.

The automobile allowance rates for 2021 are:

  • 59¢ per kilometre for the first 5,000 kilometres driven
  • 53¢ per kilometre driven after that
    In the Northwest Territories, Yukon, and Nunavut, there is an additional 4¢ per kilometre allowed for travel.

The automobile allowance rates for 2020 are:

  • 59¢ per kilometre for the first 5,000 kilometres driven
  • 53¢ per kilometre driven after that
    In the Northwest Territories, Yukon, and Nunavut, there is an additional 4¢ per kilometre allowed for travel.

The automobile allowance rates for 2019 are:

  • 58¢ per kilometre for the first 5,000 kilometres driven
  • 52¢ per kilometre driven after that
    In the Northwest Territories, Yukon, and Nunavut, there is an additional 4¢ per kilometre allowed for travel.

The automobile allowance rates for 2018 are:

  • 55¢ per kilometre for the first 5,000 kilometres driven
  • 49¢ per kilometre driven after that
    In the Northwest Territories, Yukon, and Nunavut, there is an additional 4¢ per kilometre allowed for travel.

The automobile allowance rates for 2016 and 2017 are:

  • 54¢ per kilometre for the first 5,000 kilometres driven
  • 48¢ per kilometre driven after that
    In the Northwest Territories, Yukon, and Nunavut, there is an additional 4¢ per kilometre allowed for travel.

The automobile allowance rates for 2015 are:

  • 55¢ per kilometre for the first 5,000 kilometres driven
  • 49¢ per kilometre driven after that
    In the Northwest Territories, Yukon, and Nunavut, there is an additional 4¢ per kilometre allowed for travel.

Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

If the benefit is taxable, you must withhold the following deductions. The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

The withholding and remitting requirement depends on the type of remuneration: cash , non-cash , or near-cash .

  • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

    Withhold:

    • Income tax
    • CPP
    • EI (do not withhold)

    Remit:

    • GST/HST

  • Cash: Option 2

    Withhold:

    • Income tax
    • CPP
    • EI

    Remit:

    • GST/HST - Reimbursements
    • GST/HST - Allowances (do not remit)

Report the benefit on a slip

If the benefit is taxable, you must report the following on the T4 slip.

  • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

    Report on:

    • Box 14 - Employment Income
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
    • Code 40 - Other Information

  • Cash: Option 2

    Report on:

    • Box 14 - Employment Income
    • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
    • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
    • Code 40 - Other Information
